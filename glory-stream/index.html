THE GLORY STREAM CONVOCATION

The Mandate

Glory Stream Convocation is a conventional worship gathering where the true Gospel of Jesus Christ is preached, Spirit-filled praise and worship music is unleashed, and believers are immersed in the tangible presence of God to become agents of His kingdom in their world.

We are a movement and a sacred assembly. Born from a deep hunger for authentic worship and biblical truth, Glory Stream Convocation exists to bridge the gap between the reverence of the historic faith and the freedom of Spirit-led revival.

Under the leadership of PHILPEACE, this movement is issuing a call to the nations: Come out of spiritual complacency, worship God truly and Live transformed.

The Name

GLORY

The manifest, tangible presence of God. It is the revelation of His character, His holiness, and His saving power, demonstrated fully through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

STREAM

The continuous, ongoing work and filling of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer. It represents the constant supply of God’s grace, power, and life that sustains us daily.

CONVOCATION

A formal, sovereign calling together of God's people. We do not gather by accident; we are divinely summoned by the Spirit to assemble for worship, the preaching of the Word, and mutual edification.

Our Vision

To foster a transformational worship lifestyle, becoming a generation that carries the presence and power of God into every sphere of life.

The vision goes beyond the event. We want that encounter to reshape everyday lives. A transformational worship lifestyle means that worship is no longer just an activity, but our core identity.

{Colossians 3:17, 23, Isaiah 43:7,21}

Our Mission

To cultivate an holistic culture of worship that begins in private devotion and overflows into corporate celebration, convening Spirit-filled gatherings where the true Gospel is preached and the Holy Spirit brings life.

Our Four Pillars

01 The True Gospel – Our Anchor

Jesus Christ is the foundation. We unashamedly preach Christ crucified, risen, and returning. Every song, every prayer, and every sermon points back to the cross.

02 Spirit-Filled Worship - Our Response

We prioritize authentic, heartfelt praise and intimate worship, making room for the spontaneous leading of the Spirit, prophetic ministry, and spiritual gifts, all exercised biblically.

03 Biblical Conviction - The Foundation

We are deeply rooted in the authority of Scripture. We do not chase emotional experiences; we submit our emotions and experiences to the Word of God.

04 Lifestyle Transformation - The Outcome

A true encounter with the Holy Spirit always results in transformed character, bold witness, and active compassion. We are called to be salt and light.

THE GOSPEL

What is the Gospel?

The word “Gospel” comes from the Greek word euangelion, meaning “good news.” In Christian faith, the Gospel is the good news about the life, death, resurrection, and ascension of Jesus Christ for the salvation of mankind, and the hope for the second coming of Jesus Christ. It’s the heart of Christian belief — God’s rescue (redemption) plan to redeem fallen humanity through Jesus.

1 God’s Original Design

God created the world good, perfect, and without sin. Humanity was made in God’s image (Genesis 1:26–27) to glorify, worship, and fellowship with Him. Truth: Life has purpose, and mankind was designed to live in harmony with God.

2 The Fall of Man

Sin entered the world through Adam and Eve’s disobedience (Genesis 3). Sin separated humanity from God, bringing death, suffering, and spiritual separation (Romans 3:23, Romans 6:23). Truth: All people are born in sin and are incapable of saving themselves.

3 God’s Redemptive Plan

God, in His love, promised a Savior (Genesis 3:15). Through Israel, prophets spoke of a coming Messiah who would bear sin and restore man’s relationship with God (Isaiah 53, Micah 5:2).

4 The Life of Jesus Christ

Jesus was born of a virgin (Matthew 1:23), fully God and fully man (John 1:14). He lived a sinless life, taught truth, performed miracles, and revealed God’s character (Hebrews 4:15). Truth: Jesus is God’s perfect representative to man.

5 The Death of Jesus

Jesus willingly died on the cross for the sins of humanity (1 Peter 2:24). His death satisfied God’s justice and demonstrated His love (Romans 5:8). Truth: Salvation was purchased by the blood of Christ alone.

6 The Resurrection

On the third day, Jesus rose from the dead (Luke 24:6–7). His resurrection proves His victory over sin, death, and Satan (1 Corinthians 15:3–8). Truth: The resurrection gives believers hope of eternal life.

7 Ascension & Lordship

Jesus ascended to heaven and is seated at the right hand of God (Acts 1:9–11, Ephesians 1:20–23). He reigns as Lord, intercedes for believers, and will return to judge the world (Philippians 2:9–11).

8 Salvation by Grace

Salvation is a free gift, not earned by works (Ephesians 2:8–9). It’s received through repentance and faith in Jesus Christ alone (Romans 10:9–10). Truth: Anyone can be saved by trusting in Jesus as Lord and Savior.

9 New Life & Transformation

Believers are made new, indwelt by the Holy Spirit (2 Corinthians 5:17, Galatians 5:22–23). They grow in holiness, community, and mission. Truth: The gospel transforms lives now and prepares us for eternity.

10 The Eternal Hope

Believers have the hope of eternal life with God (John 14:1–3). Jesus will return, sin will be finally defeated, and creation will be restored (Revelation 21:1–5). Truth: The gospel secures a glorious eternal future.
